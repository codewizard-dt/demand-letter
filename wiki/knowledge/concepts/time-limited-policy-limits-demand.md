# Time-Limited Policy Limits Demand (CCP §999)

relates_to::[[../sources/aaa-insurance-demand-letter-pat-donahue.md]] | relates_to::[[demand-letter.md]]

## Definition

A **time-limited policy limits demand** is a specialised demand letter subtype under California Code of Civil Procedure §999. The claimant offers to settle all claims for the insurer's full applicable policy limits, subject to a strict acceptance deadline (typically 30 days). If the insurer fails to accept within the time limit and in full compliance with all stated conditions, the insurer may face **bad-faith liability** — exposure beyond policy limits in any subsequent judgment.

## Key Characteristics

- **Expiry:** Must state a clear deadline (date and time) for acceptance. In the sample letter, expiry is June 29, 2026 at 12:00 p.m. PST.
- **Demand amount:** "All applicable bodily injury policy limits" (not a fixed dollar figure).
- **Compliance requirements:** Acceptance requires delivery of: (a) settlement draft made payable solely to claimant and law firm; (b) certified copies of all declaration pages for all operative policies; (c) a compliant release (scope-limited, no third-party releases, no indemnification of insurer/counsel); (d) insured declaration under penalty of perjury covering insurance disclosure, scope-of-employment, and ride-share status.
- **Lien handling:** Requires proof of legally perfected liens before adding any lien claimant as payee.
- **Partial/non-compliant acceptance = rejection:** Any deviation from stated terms is deemed a rejection.
