# Pre-Litigation Settlement Process (PI/Insurance)

relates_to::[[../sources/demand-letter-legal-context.md]] | relates_to::[[../sources/aaa-insurance-demand-letter-pat-donahue.md]] | relates_to::[[demand-letter.md]] | relates_to::[[time-limited-policy-limits-demand.md]]

## Overview

In personal injury insurance claims, the vast majority of cases settle before trial. The demand letter stage — where the attorney submits a formal demand package to the insurer — is where most case value is argued and most settlements are reached. Understanding this timeline is essential context for the demand letter generator: the letter is produced at a specific point in this process (after maximum medical improvement) and initiates a negotiation that may or may not end in litigation.

## The 7-Step Timeline

1. **Incident and treatment** — Claimant is injured; attorney engages and begins gathering records while claimant receives medical treatment. Attorney does not send the demand yet.

2. **Maximum medical improvement (MMI)** — Attorney waits until the claimant reaches MMI — the point at which further recovery is not expected — before drafting the demand. Sending prematurely leaves future medical damages unquantified and undervalues the claim.

3. **Demand package assembled and sent** — Attorney drafts the demand letter and assembles the full demand package: letter + medical records + bills + photographs + police report + any other evidence. The demand package is the complete evidentiary submission to the insurer.

4. **Insurer review period** — The insurer investigates the claim, reviews medical records, and assesses liability. Response time varies widely: a few weeks for straightforward cases, several months for complex ones. The insurer may request additional information.

5. **Counter-offer or denial** — The insurer responds with: (a) acceptance of the demand amount (rare); (b) a counter-offer (common — often significantly lower than the demand); or (c) outright denial. Most responses are counter-offers that open negotiation.

6. **Negotiation** — Attorney and insurer negotiate. Most PI cases settle at this stage. If a CCP §999 demand was sent, the insurer faces bad-faith liability for unreasonable rejection beyond the deadline.

7. **Litigation** — If no agreement is reached, the attorney files suit. Case proceeds to discovery, possible mediation, and trial — though most cases still settle during litigation rather than going to verdict.

## Role of the Demand Letter Generator

The generator produces the output for step 3 — the demand letter itself. The attorney provides: (a) the completed medical treatment records and bills (gathered through steps 1–2); (b) the firm's demand letter template. The generator synthesises these into a draft demand letter, enabling the attorney to skip the manual drafting effort and move directly to review and refinement. relates_to::[[../concepts/ai-document-generation.md]]

## Timing Implications

- **Do not send until MMI** — a demand sent mid-treatment will understate future care costs, weakening the claim
- **Watch the statute of limitations** — if the limitations period is tight, a tolling agreement may be needed before the demand is sent; the demand itself does not stop the clock
- **Response timeline**: attorney and client should expect weeks to months for the insurer to respond; insurer delay tactics are common
