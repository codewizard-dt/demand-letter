## Overview

A real-world California **time-limited policy-limits demand** (pursuant to CCP §999) sent by **Stalwart Law Group** (attorney Faby Rivera, Esq.) to **Interinsurance Exchange of the Automobile Club (AAA)** on behalf of personal injury claimant Patrick Donahue. Dated May 29, 2026; expiry June 29, 2026 at 12 p.m. PST.

This document serves as the canonical **template input** for the demand letter generator project — it illustrates the structure, section ordering, legal language register, and level of medical specificity expected in a complete demand letter output.

## Document Structure (Template Pattern)

The letter follows this canonical section order, which the generator must replicate:

1. **Header block** — date, delivery method, addressee (adjuster name, insurer, address), RE block (client name, claim number, insured name, date of loss), letter title, expiry notice
2. **Salutation**
3. **Liability section** — factual narrative of incident: date/time/location, traffic conditions, claimant conduct, at-fault party conduct, liability acceptance statement
4. **Client's Damages section** — detailed medical narrative: diagnoses (with ICD-level specificity), treating physicians (name, credential, practice), treatment timeline, examination findings, imaging results, future treatment recommendations
5. **Medical Expenses table** — itemised specials by provider with amounts; total to date; future medical reserve
6. **General Damages / Pain & Suffering section** — narrative linking injuries to occupational and daily-life impact; California Civil Code §1431.2(b)(2) citation
7. **Settlement Demand section** — policy-limits demand statement followed by exhaustive enumerated conditions: lien handling, payee instructions, expiry deadline and acceptance mechanics, documentation checklist (settlement draft, certified declarations pages, release terms, insured declaration)

## Settlement Condition Boilerplate

The settlement demand section contains standardised California PI demand language covering: lien perfection proof requirements; payee restrictions (client + law firm only); release scope limitations (no third-party releases, no indemnification of insurer/counsel); insured declaration requirements (scope of employment, ride-share status, other insurance disclosure, perjury consequences); and document delivery mechanics to the firm's Santa Ana office.
